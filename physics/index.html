Heat Transfer

Heat Transfer

he movement of thermal energy from one thing to another thing of different temperature. These objects could be two solids, a solid and a liquid or gas, or even within a liquid or gas.

Radiation

Transfer occurs through waves. Radiation is the process where heat emanates from an object that cannot hold any more thermal energy.

Convection

Transfer occurs through currents. Convection is the transfer of heat from one place to another by movement of a substance.

Conduction

Transfer occurs through touch. Conduction is a form of energy that manifests itself in the motion of molecules and atoms.


Real World

We can see all three forms of heat transfer in the example of a pan of liquid on a stove. The heat from the flame radiates to the pan, causing the metal to heat up the whole pan through conduction, and the liquid inside to heat up through convection (the hotter molecules move closer to the surface while the colder ones sink to the bottom of the pan).
